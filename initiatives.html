Our Education Access Initiatives

Age of Learning’s education access initiatives provide millions of children with free access to ABCmouse.com Early Learning Academy through community service programs including schools, libraries, Head Start centers, community centers, and foster care programs.

Current Initiatives


  1. Free Access for Schools

    ABCmouse.com for Schools provides ABCmouse.com Early Learning Academy free to preschool, pre-k, and kindergarten classrooms in both public and private schools throughout the U.S. ABCmouse.com for Schools also enables teachers to create and assign individualized lessons from our standards-aligned curriculum, which students can access at home through free, basic ABCmouse.com accounts. In the past school year alone, more than 50,000 teachers in the U.S. and thousands more around the world used ABCmouse.com in their classrooms. Learn more here.


  2. Free Access for Head Start Programs

    The Head Start program promotes school readiness for more than one million children age five and younger from low-income families. ABCmouse.com for Head Start offers free access to the complete ABCmouse.com Early Learning Academy for all Head Start programs, serving thousands of Head Start classrooms to date. Learn more here.


  3. Free Access for Libraries

    Libraries are often the only place where some families can access the Internet. According to the most recent American Library Association data, there are more than 1.5 billion in-person public library visits and over 340 million sessions on public access Internet computers at U.S. public libraries annually. ABCmouse.com for Libraries gives all of the more than 16,000 public libraries across the U.S. the ability to provide free access to the complete ABCmouse.com Early Learning Academy curriculum. Offering more than 350 online books, more than 450 lessons, and more than 5,000 learning activities, ABCmouse.com for Libraries provides educationally rich—and fun!—learning experiences to children regardless of their circumstances. Learn more here.

  4. Free Access for Children in the Foster Care System

    Lack of access to a consistent and high-quality education is one of the many serious challenges faced by young children in the foster care system, who often do not have the support that other children rely on to help them succeed in school. ABCmouse.com can provide educational continuity to these children, who on average change homes every six months. Age of Learning makes available free at-home access to ABCmouse.com Early Learning Academy to foster families throughout the country. Contact [fostercare@ageoflearning.com] to learn more.

  5. Community Access

    If you feel your organization serves a community purpose that would be enhanced by providing ABCmouse.com Early Learning Academy, please contact us at access@ageoflearning.com
