About Us

Age of Learning blends education best practices, innovative technology, and insightful creativity to bring learning to life for children across the U.S. and around the world.

Our content provides young learners and their families, caregivers, and teachers with highly engaging, effective learning tools, and a comprehensive curriculum that builds a strong foundation for academic success.

Reflecting our commitment to helping every child learn, Age of Learning’s Education Access Initiatives provide millions of children with free high-quality education resources through schools, libraries, community centers, Head Start centers and foster care programs.

At Age of Learning, We’re Bringing Learning to Life™

Doug DohringFounder, Chairman & CEO

Prior to founding Age of Learning in 2007, Doug Dohring was founder and CEO of NeoPets, Inc., the children’s entertainment company that built Neopets.com into a global phenomenon. By the time he sold NeoPets to Viacom in 2005, it was the #1 website in the world in time spent per user and page views per user. Doug’s experience convinced him that it was possible to create a product for children that was as engaging as NeoPets but completely dedicated to education—and Age of Learning was born. He assembled a curriculum team composed of teachers, curriculum designers, and other experts in early childhood education, and after more than four years in development, launched ABCmouse.com Early Learning Academy in 2011. Today, ABCmouse.com is the leading online education resource for young children. It is loved by kids, trusted by parents, and relied upon by teachers. Learn more about Doug Dohring.

Curriculum Board & Academic Leadership

Age of Learning collaborates with a broad range of experts in the fields of curriculum planning, instructional design, assessment, and child development in the creation of our curriculum and content for young learners. The following individuals have played lead roles in guiding our work.

 

Kimberly Oliver Burnim

Ms. Burnim, a kindergarten, first grade, and reading intervention teacher with more than a decade of classroom experience, was honored as National Teacher of the Year in 2006—one of only two kindergarten teachers to win the award in more than 60 years. Certified by the National Board of Professional Teaching Standards (NBPTS) as an early childhood generalist and currently serving on that organization’s Board of Directors, Ms. Burnim brings to ABCmouse.com a deep and forward-thinking understanding of the role that technology plays in early childhood education. In addition to strategic curriculum planning, Ms. Burnim collaborates in the design of ABCmouse.com learning activities so as to ensure that they are developmentally appropriate, incorporate effective teaching strategies, and help build a strong foundation of essential concepts and skills important for early learners.

Dr. Rebecca Palacios

A 2014 inductee into the National Teachers Hall of Fame, Dr. Palacios has more than three decades of experience in early childhood education, as well as a Ph.D. in curriculum and instruction. She is a pioneer in the field of dual language learning, and is co-founder and former vice-chairperson of the National Board of Professional Teaching Standards (NBPTS), an organization that has certified more than 100,000 teachers. Dr. Palacios is engaged in strategic curriculum planning for ABCmouse.com and in the design of age-appropriate learning activities that effectively teach essential concepts and skills to young children. In addition, she is involved in the development of site content that supports English language learning for children from households where English is not the primary language. Certified by NBPTS as an early childhood generalist, Dr. Palacios is a nationwide professional development presenter and has served on committees for the National Science Foundation, the Education Development Center in Boston, and Scholastic, Inc. Dr. Palacios has written articles on teacher professional development, early childhood education, and dual language programs.

Stephen M. Tomecek

Mr. Tomecek is the founder and Executive Director of Science Plus, Inc. Mr. Tomecek served as the Science Educational Consultant for 3–2–1 Classroom Contact produced by Children’s Television Workshop and as a curriculum specialist for both Newton’s Apple and Scholastic’s Magic School Bus. Mr. Tomecek has written over 45 nonfiction books for both teachers and children. His first book, Bouncing & Bending Light, won the 1996 American Institute of Physics science writing award, and he has written six books for the highly acclaimed Jump Into Science series published by National Geographic. Mr. Tomecek served as Associate Director for the Science Museum of Long Island and as the Science Program Supervisor for the New York Hall of Science. Mr. Tomecek has also been a writer and science consultant for Chelsea House, Scholastic, Inc., and the National Geographic Society.

Phyllis Baecker

Ms. Baecker has more than two decades of experience as an editor for several leading children’s educational publishers, including Scholastic, Inc. Throughout her career, Ms. Baecker has managed, edited, rewritten, and provided production services for numerous individual children’s books and series as well as parent’s and teacher’s guides, including the My First Steps to Reading series, Dr. Seuss and His Friends parent guides, Disney’s Wonderful World of Reading books, and the Clifford book series. Ms. Baecker’s expertise in children’s publishing helps ensure that all ABCmouse.com books represent the best editorial practices for young readers.

Dr. John Bollard

Dr. Bollard is a professional lexicographer with more than 25 years of experience. In addition to 10 years as an Editor at Merriam Webster’s, Dr. Bollard has served as Senior Editor of Cambridge Dictionary of American English (2007); editor of the Scholastic Student Thesaurus (2002) and Children’s Thesaurus (1998); Contributing Editor of the New Oxford American Dictionary (2001); and Consulting Editor for Webster’s New World Thesaurus (1996). Dr. Bollard edits all entries in the extensive ABCmouse.com glossary, and helps to ensure that the site is a rich and valuable resource for vocabulary development for young children.

Dr. Leslie Ponciano

Dr. Ponciano is a researcher and professor who has conducted 17 formal childhood development studies. She earned a B.A. in Psychology from the University of California, Irvine; an M.A. in Developmental Psychology from Teachers College, Columbia University; and a Ph.D. in Education from UCLA. At UCLA, Dr. Ponciano created early childhood education courses and directed an academic program. More recently, she created and directed the Early Childhood Education Masters program at Loyola Marymount University. She currently serves on the board of directors for Peace4Kids, a community-based organization serving foster children.
